Dr Kwame Nkrumah

A visionary Leader

Dr. Kwame Nkrumah, third from the left was the first Prime Minister and President of Ghana, having led the Gold Coast to independence from Britain in 1957

Here is a time line of Dr. Kwame Nkrumah

  • 1909 - Born in Nkroful, Gold Coast (now Ghana)
  • 1939 - Completed a Bachelor of Arts degree in economics and sociology.
  • 1939 - Enrolled in Lincoln's seminary at Philadelphia and was appointed an assistant lecturer in philosophy
  • 1942 - Initiated into the Mu chapter of Phi Beta Sigma fraternity at Lincoln University.
  • 1942 - Gained a Bachelor of Theology degree from Lincoln
  • 1943 - Earned a Master of Arts degree in philosophy and a Master of Science in education
  • 1945 - Left the United States in May and went to England, where he organized the 5th Pan-African Congress in Manchester.
  • 1947 - Invited to serve as the United Gold Coast Convention (UGCC) general secretary
  • 1948 - Addressed meetings throughout the Gold Coast and began to create a mass base for the new movement. When extensive riots occurred in February the British briefly arrested him and other leaders of the UGCC.
  • 1949 - Formed the new Convention Peoples’ Party (CPP) (CPP)
  • 1950 - Initiated a campaign of “positive action,” involving nonviolent protests, strikes, and noncooperation with the British colonial authorities and was arrested and sentenced to one year’s imprisonment
  • 1952 - Released from prison to become the prime minister of the Gold Coast due to the Gold Coast’s first general election which demonstrated the CPP had already won
  • 1957 - Became the new nation’s first prime minister when the Gold Coast and the British Togoland trust territory became an independent state within the British Commonwealth
  • 1957 - Got married to Fathia Nkrumah
  • 1960 - Ghana became a republic by a plebiscite and Nkrumah became its president
  • 1972 - Died in Bucharest, Romania
